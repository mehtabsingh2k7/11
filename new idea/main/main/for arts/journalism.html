Journalism and Media Careers and Courses

Why Choose Journalism and Media?

Journalism and Media careers offer exciting opportunities to inform, influence, and inspire people. If you love storytelling, writing, photography, or being the voice behind important issues, this is a dynamic field you can explore after Arts!

Popular Courses After Arts

Courses You Can Pursue:

  • BA in Journalism and Mass Communication
  • Bachelor of Mass Media (BMM)
  • Diploma in Broadcast Journalism
  • Diploma in Digital Media and Communication

Categories of Professions in Journalism and Media

Career Options:

  • News Reporter
  • TV Anchor
  • Photojournalist
  • Public Relations Officer

News Reporter

News Reporters gather, investigate, and report on current events. They work across print, TV, radio, and online platforms to bring real stories to the public.

Popular Courses:

  • BA Journalism
  • Diploma in News Reporting and Editing
    •

Benefits of the Profession:

  • Exciting fieldwork and chance to cover important news events.
  • Opportunities to work with leading news channels, newspapers, and digital platforms.
  • Good career growth, especially with specialization in politics, sports, or international affairs.
  • Qualification: A degree in Journalism with excellent writing and research skills.
    •

TV Anchor

TV Anchors are the faces of television news and programs. They present news stories, conduct interviews, and moderate debates with clarity and confidence.

Popular Courses:

  • Bachelor in Mass Communication
  • Diploma in Anchoring and Broadcasting
    •

Benefits of the Profession:

  • High public visibility and opportunity to build a strong personal brand.
  • Chances to work in national and international news networks.
  • Attractive salaries and fame for successful anchors.
  • Qualification: Mass Communication degree with excellent communication and public speaking skills.
    •

Photojournalist

Photojournalists tell stories through powerful images. They cover news events, wars, sports, and human interest stories through photography that speaks louder than words.

Popular Courses:

  • BA in Photography and Journalism
  • Diploma in Photojournalism
    •

Benefits of the Profession:

  • Work with newspapers, magazines, news agencies, or independently as a freelance photographer.
  • Travel to different locations and capture history as it unfolds.
  • Potential to win international awards and recognition.
  • Qualification: Journalism degree with strong photography skills and an eye for detail.
    •

Public Relations Officer

Public Relations Officers manage a company's or individual's image in the media. They create press releases, organize events, and develop communication strategies to maintain a positive reputation.

Popular Courses:

  • Bachelor of Mass Media (BMM)
  • Diploma in Public Relations and Corporate Communication
    •

Benefits of the Profession:

  • Work with top companies, PR agencies, and government organizations.
  • Opportunities to network with celebrities, politicians, and business leaders.
  • Attractive salary packages and career growth into PR management roles.
  • Qualification: Mass Communication or Public Relations degree with strong networking and communication skills.
